The Problem

Most contract problems do not start as big failures. They start as small gaps.

Most delivery teams are not short on effort. They are short on clear control.

Jobs are split across emails, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and paper. Evidence is missing at handover. Approvals slow work down. Managers spend hours chasing updates. By the time these issues show up in client meetings, complaints, payment delays, or margin reports, the damage has already started.

  • → A job sits waiting for approval.
  • → A photo is missing at handover.
  • → A return visit is booked because the first visit did not capture enough information.
  • → A manager has to chase three people to find out what is happening on a live job.
  • → A cost overrun is spotted after the job is finished.

The Review

What is the Contract Control Review?

The Contract Control Review is a structured review of how your live contract work is being controlled.

We look at your current job flow, approval process, evidence capture, reporting routine, and management visibility.

The aim is not to sell you a generic system. The aim is to show where your current operation is leaking time, control, evidence, or margin.

This is not a software demo. It is an operations-focused review with a clear findings report and a practical improvement plan at the end.

What the review covers

  • Workflow review
  • Sample job review
  • Evidence review
  • Approval review
  • Materials and scheduling review
  • KPI baseline
  • Control gap list
  • Findings report
  • Setup recommendation

Areas Reviewed

What we look at

The review is structured around the areas where housing contractors most commonly lose control of live jobs.

01

Live job visibility

How you track active jobs, stages, blockers, owners, and next actions.

Jobs stuck in progress with no clear owner or next step.

02

Delays and approvals

Where work waits for client decisions, internal approval, further information, materials, or access.

Additional works sitting unapproved while the programme drifts.

03

Evidence and handover

Whether job records contain the photos, notes, checks, certificates, and sign-off evidence needed to protect the business.

Completion claimed but final photo evidence is missing.

04

Repeat visits and rework

Where poor diagnosis, missing materials, unclear scope, or weak handover creates extra visits.

Operatives returning because first-visit notes did not capture the full issue.

05

Manager chasing load

How much time managers spend finding updates instead of managing outcomes.

Contract managers chasing WhatsApp, emails, spreadsheets, and site calls.

06

Reporting and client confidence

How well your current reports show real contract control, not surface-level activity.

Reports show completed jobs but not blockers, approval delays, or evidence gaps.

07

Commercial leakage

Where labour, materials, variations, missed extras, or late approvals create margin risk.

Extra labour used before the cost impact is visible.
